Background

Formed in 1977 in Leeds, England, Scritti Politti began as a Marxist-inspired collective. The name is a play on the Italian term "Scritti Politici" which roughly translates to "political writings". The band's early work was characterized by a deconstructed sound and politically charged lyrics.

In the early 1980s, Gartside changed the band's direction dramatically, embracing a more polished, soul and pop-influenced sound. This transformation culminated in the critically acclaimed album "Cupid & Psyche 85".

Legacy

Scritti Politti's influence extends beyond their chart success. The band's innovative approach to production and Gartside's philosophical lyrics have influenced numerous artists across different genres. Their sophisticated blend of pop, R&B, and electronic elements helped shape the sound of 1980s pop music.

Despite long periods of inactivity, Scritti Politti remains a cult favorite, with Green Gartside recognized as an innovative and thoughtful musician whose work bridges the gap between avant-garde experimentation and mainstream pop accessibility.
